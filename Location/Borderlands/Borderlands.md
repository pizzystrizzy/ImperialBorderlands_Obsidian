# Overview
Along the border between the Empire and [[The Waste]], two rivers — the [[Krysivor River|Krysivor (“Arrow River”)]] and the [[Mirmen River|Mirmen (“Black Rock”)]] — run northeast from the [[Meniri Mountains]] down to the Tëhonaurë. The region between the mountains, the ocean, and the two rivers is known as the Borderlands. The Borderlands has been contested throughout recorded history and its landscape is littered with ancient fortresses, bloodsoaked battlefields, and dread ruins, crumbling relics of the empires that once ruled there.

The Borderlands are secured by a fortified line of forts, but even when these have been fully manned, it has never been an entirely secure region. Two years ago, most of the Borderlands’ legions were sent west to confront the Skysos. Now the dangers facing the Borderlands are greater than ever. Monsters are slipping across the porous border to terrorize and plunder. Travel has grown perilous, and the border forts are becoming increasingly isolated and outmatched. The power structure of the region has begun decentralizing to local warlords and private armies.

## History
The Borderlands are dotted with the detritus of empires. In the remnants of the elder forests, elven keeps lost during the Argollëan War, two hundred years ago, lurk hidden under vine and leaf. West of the [[Mirmen River]], doughty forts constructed to quell dissent after the Krysean Rebellions of the First Century IY still face westward. Along the western bank of the [[Krysivor River|Krysivor]], a line of border forts built by Audarius XI Ulkyreus guard against the latest threat from [[The Waste]]. East of the Krysivor River are centuries-old keeps and watchtowers built to watch [[The Dark Wall]] during the Beastman Wars. The Dark Wall itself is littered with temples, tombs, and fortresses, and the Zaharan Waste is scarred with ruins of tragic grandeur
